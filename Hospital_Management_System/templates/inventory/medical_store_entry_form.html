Stock Entry — Quick Reference Guide
  1. Step 1 Item Search — Start typing the product name to search existing catalogue items. Use a barcode scanner to auto-fill from product barcodes. If the item doesn't exist, click "Add New Item" or press N.
  2. Step 1 Item Type — Select "Medicine" for drugs, "Supply" for reagents/consumables, or "Device" for equipment. Medicine-specific fields (dosage, strength, control status) appear only for medicines.
  3. Step 2 Batch Number — Copy the batch/lot number exactly from the product label. This is used for FEFO (First Expiry, First Out) tracking and recall tracing.
  4. Step 2 Expiry Date — Must be a future date. Expired stock cannot be entered. Items approaching expiry (within 30 days) will appear in dashboard alerts.
  5. Step 3 Pack Size Units — Number of individual sellable units in ONE pack. Example: a box of 100 tablets = 100 units.
  6. Step 3 Purchase Price — The amount paid for ONE pack (not per unit). The system auto-calculates unit cost = purchase price / pack size.
  7. Step 3 Target Profit Margin — Set as a percentage (0-99%). Selling price is calculated as: unit cost ÷ (1 − margin/100). A 25% margin on a 100 UGX unit cost gives a 133 UGX selling price.
  8. Step 3 Manual Override — Click "Manual price override" to set a custom selling price instead of auto-calculating from margin.
  9. Security Price Visibility — Purchase cost and profit margins are visible only on this entry screen and in Finance reports. Pharmacy staff see only selling prices. Lab and radiology technicians see no prices at all.
