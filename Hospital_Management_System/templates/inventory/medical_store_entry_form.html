Stock Entry — Quick Reference Guide
  1. Step 1 Item Identification — Enter the product name exactly as it appears on the packaging. If the item already exists in the system, it will update the existing record rather than creating a duplicate. Fill in the generic name (INN), dosage form, strength, and unit of measure.
  2. Step 1 Category & Brand — Select from existing options, or type a new one in the "new" field. You only need one or the other — not both.
  3. Step 2 Batch Number — Copy the batch/lot number exactly from the product label. This is used for FEFO (First Expiry, First Out) tracking and recall tracing.
  4. Step 2 Expiry Date — Must be a future date. Expired stock cannot be entered. Items approaching expiry (within 30 days) will appear in dashboard alerts.
  5. Step 2 Supplier — Select an existing supplier or add a new one. Supplier records help with purchase order tracking and auditing.
  6. Step 3 Pack Size Units — Number of individual sellable units in ONE pack. Example: a box of 100 tablets = 100 units.
  7. Step 3 Packs Received — How many packs arrived. Total units = Pack Size Units × Packs Received.
  8. Step 3 Purchase Price — The amount paid for ONE pack (not per unit). The system auto-calculates unit cost = purchase price ÷ pack size.
  9. Step 3 Target Profit Margin — Set as a percentage (0-99%). Selling price is calculated as: unit cost ÷ (1 − margin/100). A 25% margin on a 100 UGX unit cost gives a 133 UGX selling price.
  10. Tip Duplicate Batches — The same batch number cannot be entered twice for the same item. If you receive more of an existing batch, enter it as a new delivery with a different batch identifier.
